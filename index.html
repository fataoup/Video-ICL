Sec.2   Abstract

In-context learning for vision data has been underexplored compared with that in natural language. Previous works studied image in-context learning, urging models to generate a single image guided by demonstrations. In this paper, we propose and study video in-context learning, where the model starts from an existing video clip and generates diverse potential future sequences, each semantically guided by the prompted video demonstrations. To achieve this, we provide a clear definition of the task, and train an autoregressive Transformer on video datasets. We thoroughly analyze the effect of different datasets and represent frames as discrete tokens, and then model them by next token predictions.

Sec.4   [Rebuttal] Additional results

1. Vid-ICL on VP2 and Minecraft environment

We have conducted experiments on the VP2 and Minecraft environment. we further apply Vid-ICL as the inner generation model for an RL-agent in VP2 benchmark and Minecraft environment, where Vid-ICL produce future frames and thus generate action signals by inverse dynamics.


On VP2 [1] benchmark. VP2 contains several tasks and we pick out the Push_red task for demonstration. Vid-ICL functions as the inner video generation model within an interactive agent. In this circumstance, the action signal condition is converted to video query condition for Vid-ICL to perform in-context generation, and produced future frames are used to generate action signals by inverse dynamics. We see compared to the Svg baseline, the final state of Vid-ICL is closer to the GT goal, which demonstrate the efficacy of Vid-ICL in interactive tasks.

[1] Tian S, Finn C, Wu J. A Control-Centric Benchmark for Video Prediction.

We further show the generalization ability of Vid-ICL as a generative agent in Minecraft[2] environment. The working pipeline of Vid-ICL is similar to that in VP2 benchmark. The first top left 4 frames is the demonstration, e.g. attacking sheep in the left video. Conditioned on the scenes, Vid-ICL is also capable of generating coherent future frames and convert to attacking sheep actions in different scenarios.

[2] https://github.com/minerllabs/minerl

2. Vid-ICL on RT-1 dataset

Vid-ICL can be further applied to RT-1 dataset, which feature in containing various real robotics scenarios. Each row is a distinct sample generated by Vid-ICL. We see that Vid-ICL precisely generates frame sequences according to the demonstration. For example, Pick object in row 1 and Open drawer in row 3. This shows the in-context ability of Vid-ICL can be generalized to various senarios and applications.

3. Qualitative comparison of LWM, LVM and Vid-ICL

We compare the in-context ability of LWM(7B), LVM(7B) and Vid-ICL(ours, 1.1B). Vid-ICL mostly follows the semantic of the demonstration video clip while LWM and LVM both fail to generate frames adhere to the demonstration. This phenomenon shows that a generally pretrained video generation model is not sufficient to perform video in-context learning, which subsequently highlighting the necessity of training from scratch following Vid-ICL's paradigm.

4. Vid-ICL on video perceptual tasks

We expand Vid-ICL to perceptual task ICL scenarios. We conduct experiment on VipSeg dataset and find that Vid-ICL can smoothly transfer its ICL ability to video perceptual tasks such as video segmentation.
